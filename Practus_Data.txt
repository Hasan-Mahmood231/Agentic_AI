Chapter 1: The Core Infrastructure
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a breakthrough architectural technique that grants Large Language Models (LLMs) seamless, real-time access to external data sources. Instead of relying purely on static, frozen parameters learned during training, a RAG pipeline queries a vector database to fetch highly relevant document chunks before formatting the context prompt for the model. 

This mechanism drastically minimizes hallucinations. By presenting factual, authoritative ground-truth documents directly inside the query frame, the model shifts its operational role from a creative writer to an editor, drawing explicit facts exclusively from your local business records.

Chapter 2: Operational Troubleshooting
If your internal server throws a critical Network Error 404, it means the system was unable to find the requested resource path over the active cluster. To fix error 404 immediately, verify that your local environment configurations point to the correct staging gateway, clear your browser or application runtime cache, and restart your active docker container instance.
